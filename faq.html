1. General Information & Operations

1. What are the operating hours of the Cainta Municipal Hall? +
The Cainta Municipal Hall operates from Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. We implement a strict "No Noon Break" policy to ensure continuous public service for front-line transactions. We are closed on weekends and declared national/local holidays.
2. Where is the Cainta Municipal Hall located? +
The main government building is located at A. Bonifacio Ave., Barangay Sto. Domingo, Cainta, Rizal. You can view our exact map pinning and get directions via our Contact page.
View Location Map
3. Who is the current Mayor of Cainta? +
The current Municipal Mayor is Atty. J. Keith "Kit" P. Nieto. Under his multi-term leadership, Cainta was elevated to the status of the richest municipality in the Philippines and awarded Most Competitive Municipality multiple times.
Read the Mayor's Full Profile
4. Who is the current Vice Mayor? +
The current Vice Mayor is Hon. Ace Bernardo Servillon. He is a three-term Vice Mayor and previously served as a municipal councilor, heading critical committees such as Rules and Ordinances.
Read the Vice Mayor's Profile
5. Who are the Sangguniang Bayan Members? +
Our municipality has 8 distinguished Sangguniang Bayan (Municipal Council) members who draft and shape local legislation. They work closely with the Mayor to implement ordinances.
View the 8 Councilors Directory
6. How can I send a message directly to the Mayor's Office? +
You can easily send a direct message, request, or formal complaint using our integrated digital Contact Form. A representative from the Mayor's Action Team (MAT) monitors this daily and routes it to the appropriate department.
Open the Contact Form
7. Where can I find official news updates? +
Official announcements, class suspensions, and municipal news are released by the Public Information Office (PIO). You can follow their official Facebook page or contact them directly at 8696-2617.
View PIO Details
8. Does the LGU offer free legal assistance? +
Yes. The Municipal Legal Office provides free legal advice and assistance in drafting simple affidavits for indigent residents of Cainta who need them for acquiring municipal/social services.
Go to Legal Office
9. Where is the Information Desk? +
The physical Municipal Info Desk is located immediately at the main lobby of the Municipal Hall. For digital assistance, you can use the Cainta Chatbot floating at the bottom right of your screen!
View Info Desk details
10. How do I contact my Barangay Hall? +
We maintain an updated, comprehensive digital directory of all 7 Barangays of Cainta (San Andres, San Isidro, San Juan, San Roque, Sta. Rosa, Sto. Domingo, Sto. Niño), complete with their Punong Barangay, phone numbers, and official emails.
Access the Barangay Directory

2. Business Permits & Taxes

11. How do I apply for a new Business Permit? +
New businesses must apply at the Business Permits and Licensing Office (BPLO) on the Ground Floor. The primary requirements include: Barangay Business Clearance, Locational/Zoning Clearance from MPDC, and your DTI or SEC Registration.
View BPLO Office Details
12. When is the deadline for Business Permit Renewal? +
Business permit renewals are mandated by law to be conducted every January 1 to 20 of the year. Surcharges and penalties apply for late renewals unless an official extension is declared by the Mayor.
13. What are the requirements for a Working/Occupational Permit? +
Employees working within Cainta must secure a Mayor's Permit to Work from the BPLO. Pre-requisites include: a Health Certificate from the Municipal Health Office, a National/Local Police Clearance, and a Barangay Clearance.
14. Can I pay my Real Property Taxes (RPT) online? +
Yes! To help residents avoid long lines, the municipality has integrated an E-Payment Portal. You can assess and pay your Real Property Tax (Amilyar) and Business Taxes safely from your home.
Access the E-Payment Portal
15. Where do I pay my taxes physically? +
All physical tax payments, fee collections, and issuance of Official Receipts are handled by the Municipal Treasury Office located on the Ground Floor of the Municipal Hall.
Go to Treasury Office
16. Where can I get a Tax Declaration or Property Appraisal? +
Property appraisals, issuance of Tax Declarations, and mapping of lands/buildings are exclusively issued and managed by the Municipal Assessor's Office.
Go to Assessor's Office
17. How do I declare a business closure? +
To officially close a business and stop the accumulation of taxes, you must file a Notice of Retirement of Business at the BPLO, submit your original permits, and settle any outstanding gross receipt tax obligations at the Treasury.
18. Where do I get a Cedula (Community Tax Certificate)? +
Cedulas are issued rapidly by the Municipal Treasury Office. You only need to present a valid ID and fill out a basic information and income declaration slip at the designated window.
19. Do I need a Zoning Clearance for my business? +
Yes, a Locational/Zoning Clearance is a strict prerequisite for acquiring a Business Permit or a Building Permit to ensure your structure complies with Cainta's Comprehensive Land Use Plan.
View MPDC Directory
20. Who calibrates weighing scales in the public market? +
To protect consumers from fraud, the Municipal Treasury Office handles the mandatory annual testing, sealing, and calibration of all weights and measures used by market vendors.

3. Health, Hospital & Wellness

21. Does Cainta have a public hospital? +
Yes, the Cainta Municipal Hospital is a fully operational public health facility offering extensive medical services, out-patient checkups, laboratory testing, and 24/7 emergency care for residents.
View Hospital Services
22. Where can I get a COVID-19 vaccination or test? +
Vaccinations (primary and boosters) are distributed across our three Rural Health Units (RHUs). Free Antigen testing is continuously available at the Liwasang Bayan testing facility.
View COVID-19 Facilities
23. Where do I go for animal bite injections? +
For dog or cat bites, immediately visit the Animal Bite Treatment Center inside the Municipal Health Office for anti-rabies vaccines. For animal welfare concerns, contact the Vet Office.
Go to Health Office
24. Does the municipality offer free dental services? +
Yes, basic dental services such as tooth extractions, oral prophylaxis, and dental consultations are provided completely free of charge by the dental wing of the Municipal Health Office.
25. Are there maternity or lying-in clinics? +
Yes, the LGU operates lying-in clinics specifically equipped for normal, low-risk deliveries. Pregnant residents are encouraged to coordinate with their nearest RHU for prenatal check-ups.
26. Do you have a TB DOTS program? +
Yes, free comprehensive tuberculosis screening, x-rays, and a complete regimen of medication are available through the TB DOTS center at the Municipal Health Office.
27. How do I get a Sanitary Permit / Health Card? +
Food handlers and service workers must submit recent stool and urine laboratory results, and attend the mandatory sanitation seminar conducted weekly at the Health Office.
28. How can our barangay request Dengue fumigation? +
Fumigation, misting, and search-and-destroy requests can be routed through your Barangay Captain to the Health Office to combat dengue outbreaks during the rainy season.
29. Where can I get free medicines? +
The Municipal Pharmacy provides free maintenance and basic medicines for indigent residents. You must present a valid doctor's prescription from an RHU or the Municipal Hospital.
30. Does Cainta have a malnutrition feeding program? +
Yes, the Municipal Nutrition Office conducts regular supplemental feeding programs (typically lasting 120 days) for identified underweight children across various barangays.

4. Social Services & Identification

31. What are the requirements for a Senior Citizen ID? +
Seniors (60+ years old) must visit the OSCA Office. Bring a PSA Birth Certificate or valid ID showing age, proof of Cainta residency, a 1x1 photo, and the filled-out application form.
View ID Requirements
32. How do I get a PWD ID? +
Proceed to the PDAO Office. Requirements include a Medical Certificate stating the exact disability, a valid Government ID with a Cainta address, two 2x2 photos, and the application form.
Go to PDAO Office
33. How do I apply for a Solo Parent ID? +
Visit the MSWD Office. Bring a Barangay Certification (6 mos residency), proof of solo parent status, Income Tax Return (ITR), birth certificates of children, and a CENOMAR.
Go to MSWD Office
34. How can I ask for financial or medical assistance? +
Indigent residents may seek immediate financial aid for hospital bills, medicines, or emergency needs through the Malasakit Center or the Mayor's Action Team (MAT) located at the Municipal Hall.
View Malasakit Center
35. Is there a burial assistance program? +
Yes, the MSWD provides financial burial assistance to indigent families. Relatives must present the death certificate, a Certificate of Indigency, and a funeral contract.
36. How do I request replacement medicine booklets for PWD/Senior? +
If your purchase booklet is full or lost, replacement booklets can be requested immediately at the respective PDAO or OSCA offices by presenting your valid municipal ID.
37. Where do I report domestic violence or child abuse? +
Violence Against Women and Children (VAWC) should be immediately reported to the PNP Women and Children Protection Desk or the crisis intervention wing of the MSWD for urgent rescue and legal action.
38. Does the LGU offer AICS (Assistance to Individuals in Crisis)? +
Yes, the AICS program is facilitated locally through the MSWD, providing a safety net for individuals facing sudden emergencies like fire, eviction, or sudden hospitalization.
39. How do I get a PWD vehicle parking placard? +
Registered PWDs with mobility/orthopedic impairments can request a vehicle parking placard directly from the PDAO to access designated accessible parking slots in establishments.
40. How can a senior apply for the social pension? +
Indigent senior citizens who do not receive any pension from SSS or GSIS can apply for the DSWD Social Pension program by submitting their documents to the local OSCA office for evaluation.

5. Infrastructure & Environment

41. Where do I apply for a Building Permit? +
All structural building permits, occupancy permits, electrical permits, and architectural assessments are strictly handled by the Municipal Engineering Office to ensure public safety.
Go to Engineering Office
42. Who handles garbage collection schedules? +
The Waste Management Office oversees the daily garbage truck collection schedules, route planning, and solid waste segregation protocols for all subdivisions and barangays.
View Waste Management
43. How do I report a busted streetlight? +
Busted municipal streetlights along main roads and barangay streets can be reported to the General Services Office (GSO) or the Engineering Department for immediate bulb replacement.
44. Do I need a permit to cut a tree inside my property? +
Yes. By national law, a tree cutting permit must be secured from MENRO and the DENR before felling any mature tree, even if it is located inside private residential property.
Go to MENRO
45. How do I request drainage declogging? +
Requests for declogging of street canals, siphoning of drainage, and flood control maintenance should be forwarded to the Engineering Office, especially before the rainy season.
46. Do I need a permit to build a fence? +
Yes, erecting a perimeter wall or fence requires a Fencing Permit. This must be applied for at the Engineering Office to ensure it doesn't encroach on public sidewalks or property lines.
47. Who issues excavation permits for road digging? +
The Engineering Office issues excavation permits for private contractors, Maynilad, or residents needing to dig up municipal roads for plumbing or electrical conduit installations.
48. Where do I complain about severe noise pollution? +
Severe noise disturbances (like late-night karaoke) violating local ordinances can be reported to your Barangay officials or the Municipal Public Safety Office for immediate dispatch.
49. Does Cainta apprehend smoke-belching vehicles? +
Yes, the MENRO, in coordination with Traffic Enforcers, conducts random anti-smoke belching operations on major thoroughfares to maintain the municipality's air quality.
50. Who do I call to report stray dogs? +
Packs of stray dogs posing a risk to the community should be reported to the Municipal Veterinary Office for safe impounding and anti-rabies control.
Go to Veterinary Office

6. Transportation & Public Safety

51. How do I register or renew my tricycle franchise? +
Tricycle franchising, color-coding, and TODA regulations are processed exclusively by the Cainta Tricycle Regulatory Unit (CTRU). Operating without a franchise will result in impounding.
Go to CTRU Directory
52. What is the hotline for the Cainta Police? +
For police assistance, crime reporting, or emergencies, you can contact the Cainta PNP directly or coordinate with the 24/7 Municipal Public Safety Office dispatch.
View Public Safety Office
53. What is the hotline for the Fire Station? +
For fire emergencies, please call the local Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP) hotline at 8696-2616. Always state your exact street and nearest landmark.
54. Where do I redeem my confiscated license for a traffic violation? +
Confiscated driver's licenses can be redeemed at the Public Safety Office only upon the settlement and payment of the corresponding fine at the Municipal Treasury window.
55. Where do I complain about tricycle overcharging? +
File formal complaints regarding TODA fare matrix violations, arrogant drivers, or refusal to convey passengers at the CTRU office. Please note the tricycle's body number.
56. I left an item in a tricycle. Where do I go? +
Report the body number and TODA color of the tricycle to the CTRU or your local TODA president's outpost immediately. Most lost items are turned over to the CTRU desk.
57. Is there a curfew for minors in Cainta? +
Yes, Cainta strictly implements a curfew for minors from 10:00 PM to 4:00 AM. This is heavily enforced by the PNP and barangay tanods to protect the youth.
58. Is drinking alcohol on the streets allowed? +
No, drinking intoxicating liquor in public streets, sidewalks, and open alleys is strictly prohibited by municipal ordinance and violators will be apprehended.
59. Do seniors and PWDs get tricycle fare discounts? +
Yes, by national law and local ordinance, registered PWDs and Senior Citizens are entitled to a 20% fare discount on all local public utility tricycles upon presenting their ID.
60. How can I request CCTV footage for an accident? +
To acquire municipal CCTV footage for vehicular accidents or crimes, submit a formal request letter attached with a police blotter report to the MDRRMO Command Center.

7. Emergency & Disaster Preparedness

61. Who do I call for immediate rescue? +
For life-threatening emergencies, call the Cainta RESCUE 131 hotline at 8535-0131. They operate 24/7 with ambulances and rescue equipment on standby.
View all Hotlines
62. Where are the official evacuation centers during a typhoon? +
The MDRRMO designates specific public schools, covered courts, and the One Cainta Community Shelter depending on flood severity. Always coordinate with your barangay for the nearest safe zone.
63. How do we request relief goods after a disaster? +
Relief operations and food packs are deployed automatically to severely affected barangays and evacuation centers by the MSWD in coordination with the Mayor's Action Team.
64. How do I request a rescue boat during extreme flooding? +
Immediately call RESCUE 131. The MDRRMO has a fleet of motorized and rubber boats stationed at the command center ready for rapid deployment in flooded subdivisions.
65. What does the loud siren mean during heavy rains? +
The municipal siren is the MDRRMO's Early Warning System. A continuous siren indicates that river water levels have reached critical status and immediate evacuation is advised.
66. Does the LGU conduct earthquake drills? +
Yes, the MDRRMO actively facilitates the quarterly Nationwide Simultaneous Earthquake Drill (NSED) across all public schools, government buildings, and private businesses in Cainta.
67. Can our organization request First Aid/CPR training? +
Yes, homeowners associations and corporate offices can send a formal request letter addressed to the MDRRMO to schedule Basic Life Support (BLS) and First Aid seminars.
68. Who maintains the fire hydrants? +
Fire hydrants are maintained by Manila Water in strict coordination with the Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP) to ensure they are pressurized and operational during emergencies.
69. Does the LGU provide sandbags for residents? +
Sandbags are primarily utilized for municipal flood control on riverbanks. However, residents can request sandbag assistance from the Engineering office during imminent flood threats.
70. Who clears fallen trees blocking the road after a storm? +
The MDRRMO and MENRO rapid clearing teams are immediately dispatched post-typhoon to chainsaw and remove fallen trees or debris blocking major thoroughfares.

8. Civil Registry & Legal

71. How can I get a copy of a Birth, Marriage, or Death Cert? +
Request certified true copies of these civil registry documents at the Local Civil Registry Office (LCRO). You must bring a valid ID and pay the processing fee at the Treasury.
Go to LCRO Directory
72. How do we apply for a Marriage License? +
Couples must first attend the Pre-Marriage Counseling seminar at the MSWD. Afterward, submit the counseling certificate along with your birth certificates/CENOMAR to the LCRO.
73. How do I file for delayed registration of birth? +
For late registrations, you need a PSA Negative Certification, a Baptismal Certificate, and a joint affidavit of two disinterested persons to process the application at the LCRO.
74. Does the LGU provide free notary services? +
The Municipal Legal Office provides free notarization exclusively for simple affidavits (like Affidavit of Loss) needed by indigent residents acquiring specific LGU services.
75. How do I process the legitimation of a child? +
Parents who married *after* their child's birth can legitimize the child's status by filing a joint Affidavit of Legitimation at the LCRO where the birth was registered.
76. How do I correct a misspelled name on my birth certificate? +
Under RA 9048, you can file a petition for the correction of a clerical or typographical error directly at the LCRO without needing a judicial court order.
77. Where can I get a copy of a local ordinance? +
Certified copies of approved municipal ordinances and resolutions can be requested from the Sangguniang Bayan Secretariat or the Legal Office for legal or academic purposes.
78. How long is a Barangay Clearance valid? +
Unless specified otherwise for a specific transaction, a standard Barangay Clearance is generally valid for six (6) months from its date of issuance.
79. Do you issue National Police Clearances? +
Yes, the Cainta Municipal Police Station is equipped to process, capture biometrics, and issue the integrated National Police Clearance.
80. Is my personal data secure with the LGU? +
Absolutely. The municipality strictly adheres to the Data Privacy Act of 2012, ensuring all physical and digital records are protected against unauthorized access.
Read our Privacy Policy

9. Education, Youth & Jobs

81. What courses are offered at One Cainta College? +
One Cainta College provides full bachelor's degrees in Tech-Voc Teacher Education, Entrepreneurship, and Information Systems, alongside highly sought-after IT and Office Management Diploma programs.
View College Information
82. Does the municipality offer scholarships? +
Yes, comprehensive educational grants and financial assistance for deserving students are facilitated through the Youth Development Office (CYDO) and the Mayor's Office.
83. Does the LGU offer job placement help? +
Yes, the Public Employment Service Office (PESO) frequently hosts mega job fairs and assists residents with job matching for both local and overseas employment opportunities.
Go to PESO Directory
84. Is there a summer job program for students? +
Yes, PESO runs the Special Program for Employment of Students (SPES) during summer breaks, giving students a chance to earn money for tuition by working in LGU offices.
85. How do I enroll in the Alternative Learning System (ALS)? +
ALS enrollment for out-of-school youth and adults is coordinated through the DepEd District Office and the education committees of your respective barangay.
86. Is there a public library in Cainta? +
Yes, the Cainta Municipal Library is open to all residents, students, and researchers providing a quiet space for reading, studying, and free internet access for academics.
87. How do I enroll my child in a public Daycare? +
Enrollment for public Child Development Centers (Daycare) is handled directly by the MSWD workers stationed at your local barangay hall before the school year begins.
88. How do I reserve the One Cainta Arena? +
Reservations for municipal sporting facilities, including the One Cainta Arena and public basketball courts, are managed by the Sports Development Office to prevent schedule conflicts.
89. How do I register a youth organization? +
Local youth organizations and fraternities must be accredited through the Local Youth Development Office (CYDO) to participate in municipal events and acquire funding support.
90. Does the LGU have an anti-drug program? +
Yes, the Cainta Anti-Drug Abuse Council (CADAC) handles community symposiums, drug-clearing operations, and facilitates rehabilitation assistance for drug surrenderers.

10. Culture, Tourism & E-Services

91. What are the best tourist spots in Cainta? +
Top landmarks include the Museo de Cainta, One Cainta Arena, Oval Park, the historic ROTC Grounds, and the pioneering Sta. Lucia Mall.
Explore the Tourist Carousel
92. What is the history of the Sepoys in Cainta? +
Indian soldiers (Sepoys) settled in Cainta after the Seven Years' War in 1762. Their descendants remain prominent today, deeply influencing our local culture and famous delicacies like Bibingka.
Read our Full History
93. Is there an entrance fee for Museo de Cainta? +
No, entrance to the municipal museum is completely free for all visitors, students, and tourists wishing to learn about the rich tapestry of our town's past.
94. When is the Sumbingtik Festival? +
The Sumbingtik Festival—a portmanteau of Suman, Bibingka, and Latik—is celebrated grandly every December 1 alongside the town's founding anniversary.
95. Where can I see the municipality's financial reports? +
In strict compliance with DILG transparency directives, all our annual budgets, procurement plans, and financial reports are publicly available for download on our portal.
View Full Disclosure Board
96. Will I get a digital receipt for online payments? +
Yes, an electronic official receipt (e-OR) will be sent to your registered email address immediately upon a successful transaction in the E-Payment Portal.
97. Does the LGU offer assistance for small cooperatives? +
Yes, the Cooperative & Development Office provides foundational seminars, legal accreditation assistance, and livelihood programs for aspiring local cooperatives.
View Coop Office
98. Can I adopt a dog from the municipal pound? +
Yes! We strongly encourage adoption. The Municipal Veterinary Office regularly rehabilitates impounded pets and puts them up for adoption to loving homes.
99. Is Cainta integrated with the eGov PH app? +
Yes, several local LGU services, digital ID integrations, and VaxCert verifications are accessible and streamlined via the national eGov App platform.
100. I still have a question not listed here. Who can I ask? +
You may approach the physical Municipal Info Desk at the main lobby, use our Contact Form, or interact with our digital Chat Assistant floating on your screen right now!
